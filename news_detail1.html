Ed Sheeran Chooses To Retire When Convicted Of Plagiarizing Songs.

Andrea Smith

3 May

Ed Sheeran vowed that if found guilty in the case of alleged copyright infringement over Thinking Out Loud of Marvin Gaye's Let's Get It On song, he would stop making music.

"If that happens [provided guilty], I will stop," said Ed Sheeran based on the Daily Mail report, quoted from Insider on Wednesday (3/5).

"I feel offended because I devoted my whole life to being a performer and songwriter, but someone ruined it," he added.

Ed Sheeran was sued by Kathryn Townsend as the daughter of Ed Townsend, who wrote the song Let's Get It On with Marvin Gaye.

As well as Kathryn, Helen McDonald's sister, and the estate of Townsend's ex-wife, Cherrigale Townsend, are listed as plaintiffs in the "Thinking Out Loud" case.

When Sheeran was present at the inaugural trial which was held in Manhattan federal court, Tuesday (25/4) US time, the trial opened with the playback of the songs Thinking Out Loud and Let's Get it On.

Ben Crump, attorney for Ed Townsend's heirs who was a co-songwriter with Marvin Gaye, showed a video corroborating the allegations. They accused Ed Sheeran with Warner Music Group and Sony Music Publishing of being in debt for allegedly plagiarizing the song Let's Get it On.

Ben Crump later accused the video of being a "confession" that Ed Sheeran had plagiarized. However, Ed Sheeran denied this accusation in his first testimony. He admitted that he had mash up a number of songs in various previous concerts.

The 32-year-old soloist also explained that the case was not a copyright infringement. This is because there are many songs that have the same chords without the intention of plagiarizing them. Ed Sheeran later insisted he had no intention of plagiarizing a Marvin Gaye song, as the plaintiffs allege.
