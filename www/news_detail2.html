Confirmation of Coldplay Concert in Indonesia Called Announced in May.

John Brewster

2 May

Coldplay fans in Indonesia have been worried since the last few weeks. The news that Coldplay will hold a concert in Indonesia is still circulating, it is even said that it will be announced in the near future.

Minister of Tourism Sandiaga Uno said some time ago that the certainty of a Coldplay concert in Indonesia would be announced in the first or second week of May.

"It will be delivered in the first or second week of this month (May)," said Sandiaga in Jakarta, Friday (21/4).

Sandiaga is reluctant to go ahead with related parties regarding the certainty of a Coldplay concert in Indonesia. Even so, he gave a leak that the progress of Coldplay's plan to perform in Indonesia was going well.

"Yesterday's Coldplay concert, the committee gave the latest information, even though there is good news, but friends, be patient, because we want everything to be neat." said Sandiaga Uno.

Sandiaga emphasized that Indonesia is ready to host major events. Sandi said world-class events could also have a positive impact on Indonesia's economic revival after the Covid-19 pandemic.

Sandiaga Uno was in the spotlight after previously accidentally uploading a number of videos to his social media containing meetings with promoters who said Coldplay would be having a concert in Indonesia.

Even so, there has been no confirmation from any promoters regarding the Coldplay concert in Jakarta. However, Coldplay is considered by its fans in Indonesia to have provided a number of codes.

One of them was on Saturday (29/4) when the Coldplay account responded to Indonesian fans watching their concert documentary which was shown in theaters.

Despite Coldplay's certainty that there is no new moon for concerts in Indonesia, Pelita Harapan University (UPH) concert and festival management academic Yosia Revie Pongoh believes that Indonesia must improve in terms of concert and crowd management.

In Revie's opinion, promoters should not only prioritize from a business perspective, but also from experience, service, and the assurance that the concert will run safely. This reflects the many complaints from the audience about concerts by foreign musicians recently.

"At that time he [Coldplay] toured the Philippines, Thailand, but didn't get here. That was PR for us too. Sometimes promoters don't think about the impacts," said Revie.
